INDIAN FESTIVALS

India is a land of diverse cultures and traditions, and festivals are an important part of life. They bring people together and spread joy, happiness, and unity among different communities.
Some popular Indian festivals include Diwali, the festival of lights; Holi, the festival of colors; and Pongal, a harvest festival celebrated in South India. Each festival has its own traditions, foods, and cultural significance.

Festivals of India:
India is known as a land of festivals,
where people from various religions and cultures live together in
harmony. These celebrations act as a pause from busy lives, breaking
the monotony and allowing people to share love, respect, and happiness.
Major festivals like Diwali, Holi, Eid, and Christmas are celebrated
with great fervor, bringing people together regardless of their background.
These occasions are marked by special rituals, traditional foods, and
family gatherings, showcasing India’s "unity in diversity".
