Classical Dance..

Indian classical dance comprises eight officially recognized forms by
the Sangeet Natak Akademi and the Ministry of Culture, rooted in
ancient treatises like the Natya Shastra.

The Eight Classical Dance Forms of India
Bharatanatyam (Tamil Nadu): Characterized by fixed upper torsos, bent legs, and highly expressive facial expressions, this highly spiritual form highlights poses resembling ancient temple sculptures.
Kathak (Uttar Pradesh): Known for its rapid, rhythmic footwork (tatkar), striking chakkars (spins), and fluid, storytelling abhinaya traditionally detailing mythological narratives.
Kathakali (Kerala): A spectacular, highly stylized classical drama form characterized by elaborate, colorful makeup, intricate masks, and detailed costumes portraying both heroic and demonic characters.
Kuchipudi (Andhra Pradesh & Telangana): A dynamic, fluid form that combines dramatic storytelling with swift, athletic movements, historically known for its intricate footwork on a brass plate.
Manipuri (Manipur): Distinguished by graceful, floating movements and lyrical grace, primarily focusing on the devotional love of Radha and Krishna.
Mohiniyattam (Kerala): The "dance of the enchantress," performed exclusively by women. It is known for its gentle, swaying movements, graceful footwork, and soft, lyrical storytelling.
Odissi (Odisha): Famed for its independent movements of the head, chest, and pelvis, characterized by fluid, sinuous postures and striking tribhanga (three-bend) stances.
Sattriya (Assam): A monastic dance-drama tradition that blends storytelling and spiritual devotion, historically originating from the Vaishnavite monasteries of Assam.
