History of Kuchipudi

Kuchipudi is a classical dance-drama from Andhra Pradesh, India, originating from the village of Kuchipudi.
It developed from ancient Bhagavata Mela traditions (circa 3rd century BCE) and was formalized in the 17th century by Siddhendra Yogi, who created Bhama Kalapam.
Originally, it was a traveling, all-male performance focusing on Hindu mythology.

Vempati Chinna Satyam (1929–2012) was a legendary Indian dancer
and guru who revolutionized the Kuchipudi dance form. Born in Andhra Pradesh, he brought the dance from rural, male-only traditions
to the global stage, earning it classical recognition. He established the Kuchipudi Art Academy in Madras (1963),
choreographing 15+ dramas and training prominent artists, including Hema Malini and Vyjayanthimala, earning him the Padma Bhushan.
