The History of Carnatic Music.....

Carnatic music is a South Indian classical tradition with ancient roots in Vedic, Tamil, and temple traditions, flourishing after the 15th-century Bhakti movement.
It became a distinct style during the Vijayanagara Empire, later shaped by Trinity composers (Tyagaraja, Dikshitar, Sastri) and formalized by Purandara Dasa.
Raga & Tala: Defined by melodic structures (raga) and rhythmic cycles (tala).
Composers: Beyond the Trinity, early composers like Tallapaka Annamacharya, Narayana Tirtha, and Kshetragna contributed to its development.
Modern Era: Moved from temples to concert halls (Sabhas) during the late 19th/20th

Ancient Roots (Vedic era–4th Century AD): Foundational principles originated from Samaveda chanting and ancient Tamil music (Silapathikaram).

The Father of Carnatic Music: Purandara Dasa (1484–1564) codified the teaching system by defining the Malavagowla scale and creating beginner exercises.
